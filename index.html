01 // About Me

Background & Vision

I'm Madhu Kiran Gajula, a Master's student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Binghamton University (SUNY) with a GPA of 3.7/4.0, currently working as a Graduate Research Assistant in the SPIR Program. My academic and professional journey spans RF/microwave systems, antenna engineering, power electronics, and PCB design.

In the SPIR research lab, I design and fabricate high-gain compact microstrip patch antenna arrays operating across S-, Ku-, and Ka-bands, integrating Artificial Magnetic Conductor (AMC) ground planes for enhanced performance. I also work on LNA/PA circuit design using ANSYS HFSS, LTspice, and MATLAB, and perform full simulation-to-hardware correlation through S-parameter analysis.

Prior to my graduate studies, I worked as a Power Electronics Engineer at Sagar Semiconductors in Hyderabad, where I led the full design lifecycle of a 12 V / 48 Ah Battery Management System and an isolated 48 V-to-12 V DC-DC converter — from schematic capture through board bring-up and customer acceptance testing. My career goal is to contribute to next-generation RF front-end systems, antenna arrays, and wireless communication platforms.
